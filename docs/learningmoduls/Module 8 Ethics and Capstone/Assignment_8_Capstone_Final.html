02. Technology Architecture

The LFW-Agent is a voice-first AI system integrated into the existing call centre platform. It leverages an LLM for natural language understanding and empathy, but relies on a deterministic Rule Engine for all financial decisions to prevent prompt injection and ensure policy compliance.

Core Components

Telephony Integration

Integration with Asterisk/BroadSoft/Twilio-class CCaaS platforms for call routing, queueing, and seamless escalation to human agents when needed.

STT & TTS

Cloud-based Speech-to-Text and Text-to-Speech supporting Singapore English and regional accents for natural, conversational interactions.

LLM Dialogue Engine

Cost-efficient LLM (GPT-4o-mini tier) for routine queries; premium tier reserved for complex billing disputes. Never directly executes financial transactions.

Rule Engine

Deterministic business logic enforcing waiver policies: eligibility thresholds, max amounts, frequency limits. LLM proposes; Rule Engine validates and executes.

Assignment 2 & 3 Feedback Applied

04. Security & Risk Management

Security follows the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover) aligned with Singapore's PDPA and the Model AI Governance Framework for Agentic AI.

NIST Framework Application

Identify

Asset Inventory: Document all LFW-Agent components, data flows (IVR → STT → LLM → Rule Engine → BSS), and access points.

Risk Classification: High-impact (monetary decisions), bounded scope (strict thresholds).

Protect

Access Boundaries: Agent can only approve waivers ≤ S$20, once per 12 months; anything higher escalates.

Prompt Injection Defense: LLM proposes action; Rule Engine validates before execution.

Detect

Anomaly Monitoring: Real-time dashboards tracking waiver volume, patterns, and unusual spikes.

QA Sampling: 10–15% weekly review of AI decisions for accuracy and fairness.

Respond

Incident Playbooks: If anomaly detected, disable auto-waivers, notify CISO/Finance/Legal, review transactions.

Circuit Breaker: Automatic pause if waivers exceed threshold.

PDPA Compliance (Singapore Data Privacy)

Data Protection Measures

  • Data Minimization: Only pull fields required (balance, history)—never full NRIC or card numbers into LLM.
  • Consent & Transparency: IVR preamble: "Your call may be handled by an AI agent and recorded."
  • Encryption: AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit; role-based access for logs.
  • Retention Limits: Logs retained only as long as necessary (180 days unless dispute).
  • Explainability: AI provides clear reason for waiver decision to customer.
  • Human Review Option: Customers can request human agent review if dissatisfied.

07. Governance & Compliance

Operating under Singapore's stringent regulatory environment: PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act), IMDA's Model AI Governance Framework for Agentic AI (MGF), and Telecom sector rules.

Governance Structure

AI Risk Steering Committee

Members: CTO (chair), CISO, Chief Legal/Privacy Officer, Head CX, CFO, external AI ethics advisor

Cadence: Monthly during pilot; quarterly at maturity

Ownership & Accountability

Product Owner: LFW-Agent commercial outcomes

DPO: PDPA compliance

CISO: Cyber risk and resilience

MGF Risk Classification & Testing

Testing Pipeline

  • Sandbox Environments: Use masked customer data compliant with PDPA
  • A/B Testing: Randomized traffic splits in pilot to isolate AI vs. human performance
  • Bias Testing: Monitor waivers by language, geography, tenure to detect fairness issues
  • Pre-Deployment Checks: PII leakage detection, accuracy on curated test sets

Documentation & Audit Trail

  • AI Ethics Policies: PDPA consent mechanisms, explainability requirements
  • Technical Design Records: Data lineage, model versioning, API integrations
  • Testing Evidence: Sandbox results, A/B test reports, bias analysis
  • Operational Logs: Immutable audit trails (encrypted, access-controlled)

Continuous Compliance

  • Real-Time Monitoring: Dashboards tracking agent behavior against guardrails
  • Quarterly Gap Analysis: Compare controls to MGF/PDPA requirements
  • Semi-Annual Mock Audits: Simulate regulatory inspection
  • Incident Response: PDPA breach notification within 3 days

08. Key Performance Indicators

Five core KPIs with clear ownership, targets, and decision thresholds to measure success and guide scaling decisions.

1. AI Late Fee Waiver Containment Rate (FCR)
Operational

Definition: Percentage of late fee waiver requests fully handled by AI without human intervention.

Owner: Head of Customer Service Operations

Targets: Crawl 30–40% → Walk 60–70% → Run 75–85%

Threshold: If FCR drops below 50% for 4 weeks, pause expansion and investigate.

2. Average Handle Time (AHT)
Efficiency

Definition: Average duration of AI-handled late fee calls.

Owner: Contact Centre Performance Manager

Targets: Baseline 8–10 min → Walk 4–5 min → Run 3–4 min

Consideration: Must be balanced with CSAT and fairness; never sacrifice quality for speed.

3. Customer Satisfaction (CSAT)
Experience

Definition: Post-call rating (1–5 scale) and sentiment analysis.

Owner: Head of Customer Experience

Targets: Crawl 70%+ → Walk 78% → Run ≥85% (parity with human agents)

Threshold: If AI CSAT is ≥5 points lower than human CSAT, pause rollout and review.

4. Financial Impact – Cost & Revenue Protection
Financial

Definition: Total waiver cost vs. baseline; ratio of policy-compliant waivers.

Owner: Finance Director (Consumer Business)

Target: Maintain or reduce total waiver cost while not increasing complaints or churn.

Purpose: Ensures AI isn't over-generating waivers just to maximize CSAT.

5. Escalation Rate & Quality
Learning

Definition: % of AI calls escalated to humans; % resolved on first human contact.

Owner: Billing & Collections Manager

Targets: Escalation Rate 50% (Crawl) → 30% (Walk) → 20–25% (Run); Quality >85%

Use: High escalation or poor quality blocks further automation until fixed.
